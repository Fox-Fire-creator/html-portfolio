About Me

Hi there! I’m a 13-year-old tech enthusiast with a deep passion for robotics, electronics, programming, and science. Ever since I was 6, I’ve been experimenting with circuits, sensors, and coding—starting with basic projects and eventually building complex systems that solve real-world problems. My journey has been full of curiosity, creativity, and hands-on learning.

I’ve designed and built a wide range of projects including automatic night lamps, water level indicators, battery level monitors, Bluetooth-controlled robots, gesture-based devices, and smart home systems. These projects have helped me understand everything from basic electronics and soldering to microcontroller programming, circuit design, and real-time data processing.

At age 10, I discovered my love for programming. Since then, I’ve learned Python, HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and C. I use these skills to create clean and responsive websites and to automate tasks with code. Whether it’s a functional front-end web interface or a hardware-software integration with Arduino, I enjoy making things that work—and look—great.

I’m especially interested in how different technologies interact. From coding logic to sensor data collection, I like connecting physical systems with digital solutions. I’ve also explored topics like AI basics, game development, and computer architecture to deepen my understanding of the tech world.

Outside of building and coding, I love research. I often spend hours diving into complex topics across physics, chemistry, and emerging technologies—because I don’t just want to build stuff, I want to understand how and why it works.

I'm also a gamer and video creator. At age 7, I started a YouTube channel where I recorded, edited, and uploaded gaming videos all by myself. It taught me early skills in editing, audio, scripting, and presentation. The channel is still live, and I plan to return to it with tech builds, coding tutorials, and game-related content.

In short, I’m a maker, coder, and curious learner who thrives on experimenting, building, and sharing knowledge. Whether it’s on a breadboard, a code editor, or a blank canvas for an idea—I’m always working on something exciting. I’m open to freelance work, collaborations, and anything that challenges my creativity and helps me grow.
